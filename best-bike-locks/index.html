Quick answer

The Kryptonite New York Fahgettaboudit Mini U-Lock is our top pick for anyone who needs maximum security in a high-theft area.

The Abus Granit X-Plus 54 is a nearly identical runner-up with a slightly friendlier mounting system, while the Master Lock Street Cuff offers surprising protection at a budget-friendly price.

A bike lock is the only thing standing between you and a stolen ride, so choosing the right one matters far more than you might think. The best lock for you depends on where you park, how much weight you’re willing to carry, and how much you’re comfortable spending. The most secure options are heavy and expensive, but they’re the only choice if you leave your bike unattended in a city for hours. Lighter locks work fine for quick errands or low-crime neighborhoods, but every compromise on security is a calculated risk. When shopping, look for two things above all else: the lock’s material and its locking mechanism. Hardened steel shackles and double-locking disc detainers are the gold standard; cheap padlock-style cylinders can be shimmed in seconds. Weight and portability are the next trade-offs, a high-security U-lock might weigh several pounds, while a folding lock or cable combo is far easier to stow. Ignore any lock that doesn’t come with a mounting bracket, because a lock you leave at home is useless.
