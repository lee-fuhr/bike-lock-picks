How we choose our picks

We never claim to have run timed cutting tests ourselves. Instead, we sift through years of owner forums, published reviews from security experts, and real-world theft reports to see which locks actually survive sustained attacks. We look for long-term reliability complaints, things like seized shackles, broken keyways, or rust that weakens the steel, and weigh that against the lock’s price and portability. We also study what actually defeats each lock: whether it is bolt cutters, leverage, or an angle grinder. A lock that delays a thief for five minutes is worthless if it can be picked in ten seconds, so we prioritize designs with proven attack resistance and solid construction materials, not just flashy marketing claims.
